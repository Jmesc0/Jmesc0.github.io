Hollowed Noskris Mask

Not much is known about the hollowed noskris. They spend much of their time in large caves and caverns, coming outside only to hunt prey occasionally. When they do go on an occasional hunt, or when other creatures venture into their territory, they are extremely deadly, even being credited as one of the most likely causes for the disappearance of Meili 3. As such, their masks and other remains are often hard to come by and highly valuable. Only a handful of either fully grown or fully intact masks can be found in rare collections.

Two-Toed Salarango Footprint

An actual mold of a footprint taken from the Meili Mission can be seen above. Initially it was unknown what creature had made the footprint. The impression was most likely a baby Two-Toed Salarango footprint that was found, as the imprint and location of the footprint match that of the adult, Two-Toed Salarangos from the same region.

Nexo-Ammonite Fossil

Like Terra and Europa, Neumonde features a host of life preserved in fossils. While the field of astro-paleontology often poses more questions than gives answers with the reason for the similarities between the ammonite and nexo-ammonite being among one of the more puzzling mysteries. Being extremely similar to ammonite fossils on Terra and xeno-ammonite fossils found on Europa, the nexo-ammonite seems to demonstrate how life managed to not just start but, also evolve nearly identically on planets and systems with no known interaction with each other. This remains one of the greatest mysteries of Neumonde and of astro-paleontology.
